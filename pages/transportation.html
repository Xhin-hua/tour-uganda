    OVERVIEW OF THE ENTIRE TRANSPORTATION SECTOR

    Uganda's transportation network plays a vital role in connecting people, goods, and services across the country and beyond. The system comprises various modes, including roads, railways, waterways, and air transport.

    Choose to travel like a local on a ZUGUUmotor cycle

    Road Network:

    Uganda's road network is the most extensive and widely used mode of transportation. The country has over 70,000 kilometers of roads, with a significant portion being paved. The road network connects major towns, cities, and rural areas, facilitating the movement of goods and people.

    Railway System:

    Uganda's railway system is another crucial mode of transportation, particularly for goods and bulk cargo. The country has a railway network that connects to neighboring countries, including Kenya, Tanzania, and Rwanda. The railway system plays a vital role in facilitating trade and commerce. .

    Water Transport:

    Lake Victoria, Lake Albert, and Lake Kyoga are Uganda's major water bodies, providing opportunities for water transportation. Ferries and boats operate on these lakes, facilitating the movement of people and goods.

    Air Transport:

    Uganda has several airports, with Entebbe International Airport being the primary gateway to the country. The airport serves as a hub for regional and international flights, connecting Uganda to the world.

    Despite the progress made in Uganda's transportation system, there are still challenges to be addressed. These include:

    • - Infrastructure maintenance and upgrade
    • - Traffic congestion in urban areas
    • - Safety concerns
    • - Limited railway network
