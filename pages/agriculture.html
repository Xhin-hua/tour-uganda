    WHY INVEST IN AGRICULTURE

    Agriculture in Uganda is a vital sector that plays a significant role in the country's economy. Here's an overview of the sector, including the budget allocation for 2025/26

    The Ugandan government has allocated Shs 1.86 trillion to boost agro-industrialization in the 2025/26 financial year. This budget focuses on research, irrigation, mechanization, value addition, and export growth. Key areas of investment include:

    • Research and Development
      Developing agricultural technologies that increase productivity, such as water-saving cultivation technology, drought-resistant varieties, and integrated pest, disease, and weed control technologies.
    • Irrigation
      Expanding water capacity for agriculture to 54.76 million cubic meters, completing seven large-scale irrigation schemes, and constructing 145 solar-powered irrigation systems
    • Mechanization
      Establishing five zonal mechanization centers, distributing 260 tractors, 750 power tillers, and two dredging machines to farmer groups, and training 2,150 youth and women as operators, artisans, and mechanics.

    Key Intiatives

    1. Agricultural Credit Facility
      The government has introduced a Shs 175 billion interest-free credit facility accessible through Post Bank, Housing Finance Bank, and Pride Microfinance to support large-scale agriculture.
    2. Value Addition
      Eleven agro-processing and storage facilities have been constructed to enhance market access and value addition for crops like maize, rice, coffee, and oil seeds.
    3. Export Growth
      Uganda has dispatched its first consignments of coffee and milk to Serbia and Algeria, with potential earnings of USD 500 million from milk exports.
