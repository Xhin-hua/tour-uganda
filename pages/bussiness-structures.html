    UGANDA

    THE PEARL OF AFRICA

    OVERVIEW OF THE ENTIRE BUSINESS STRUCTURE.

    Based on the Uganda's budget for 2025/26, here's an overview of the structure of the economy, highlighting opportunities and challenges for investors.

    KEY SECTORS

    • Agriculture

      A significant sector, contributing to food security and export earnings. Foreign investors are attracted to agro-processing, irrigation, and mechanization.

    • Industry

      Manufacturing, particularly in textiles, pharmaceuticals, and construction materials, offers opportunities for local and foreign investment.

    • Services

      Tourism, finance, and ICT sectors are growing, attracting investors interested in hospitality, banking, and technology.

    INVESTMENT OPPORTUNITIES

    Foreign Investors

    Attracted to sectors like:

    • - Oil and gas exploration - Mining
    • - Infrastructure development (roads, energy, water)
    • - Agriculture (agro-processing, irrigation)

    Local Investors*

    Focus on:

    • - Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)
    • - Agriculture (crop farming, livestock)
    • - Real estate and construction
    • - Retail and trade

    SOME ECONOMIC CHALLENGES

    • Inflation: Managed by the Bank of Uganda, inflation affects purchasing power and investment decisions.
    • Exchange Rates: Fluctuations impact trade and investment, particularly for foreign investors.
    • Infrastructure: Development challenges, such as road conditions and energy supply, affect business operations.

    GOVERNMENT INITIATIVE

    • Tax Incentives*: Offered to attract investment in key sectors, such as agriculture and manufacturing.
    • Infrastructure Development*: Investments in roads, energy, and water infrastructure to support business growth.
    • Business Support*: Initiatives to promote SMEs, entrepreneurship, and innovation. forexample the government is providing long loans with low interest rates to people"Parish Development Money (PDM)"

    INVESTMENT CLIMATE

    Regulatory Framework*: Uganda has laws and regulations to protect investors and promote business growth.

    Investment Promotion*: Agencies like the Uganda Investment Authority (UIA) support investors and promote investment opportunities.
